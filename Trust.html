Introduction


In the game of trust, there is a dilemma. In front of you is a machine: if you put a coin in the machine, the other player gets three coins
and vice versa. You both can either choose to COOPERATE (put in coin), or CHEAT (don't put in coin).

In the repeated game of trust (also known as Iterated Prisoner's Dilemma), it's important that neither player knows when the last round is. This is because the last round, both players would know their action has no consequence, so they'd both cheat. But that means in the second-last round, their actions can't change the next round, so they'd also both cheat. But that means in the third-last round... etc
